Remote AFISO VR Workplace

Professional VR Application Development

I worked on this project during my Co-op at the German Aerospace Center in Braunschweig, Germany as the sole developer. The Remote AFISO project was a prototype for an extension of the existing "Remote Tower" concept. This is a concept where the air traffic service at an airport can be supported or managed from a location other than the control tower. The goal of this concept is to be able to support airports during busy times with remote air traffic controllers, and it has already been successfully implemented at many airports around the world.

Normally, the Remote Tower requires a 360 degree array of cameras, plus a great deal of monitors and equipment in the Remote Tower Center itself. The goal of this project was to develop a prototype of a low-cost VR version for use by Aerodrome Flight Information Service Officers (AFISOs) at smaller airports. An AFISO differs from an ATC in that they do not provide takeoff clearance, but weather and hazard information, so the Remote AFISO Workplace would only need to keep track of incoming/outgoing flights, and have a view of the airport.
